PartialEdit: Identifying Partial Deepfakes in the Era of Neural Speech Editing

Interspeech 2025

You Zhang*1, Baotong Tian*1, Lin Zhang2, Zhiyao Duan1,
1Audio Information Research Lab, University of Rochester
2Speech@FIT, Brno University of Technology
*Equal Contribution

Neural speech editing enables seamless partial edits to speech utterances, allowing modifications to selected content while preserving the rest of the audio unchanged. This useful technique, however, also poses new risks of deepfakes. To encourage researches on detecting such partially edited deepfake speech, we introduce PartialEdit, a deepfake speech dataset curated using advanced neural editing techniques. We explore both detection and localization tasks on PartialEdit. Our experiments reveal that models trained on traditional partially spoofed audio fail to detect partially edited speech generated by neural speech editing models. We also provide insights into the artifacts model learned on detecting codec-processed, partially edited deepfakes. Finally, these findings motivate our argument for a clearer definition of deepfake as content-edited speech segments regardless of the codec processing. We hope that our proposed dataset, experiments, and perspectives contribute to the development of partial deepfake detection research.
